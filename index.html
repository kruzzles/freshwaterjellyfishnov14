SECTION 1

    The species that I will be focusing on for this project is the freshwater jellyfish (specifically the craspedacusta sowerbii). They have existed on earth for up to 600 million years, they were considered an endangered species in 2002. Humans hunt some edible species of jellyfish. Jellyfish is used in some dishes including salads and sushi however, scientists have said jellyfish are not a significant item on the seafood menu as they are made up of 95% water and have very little calories. Even though they aren't a very good source of calories there are other possible benefits which include having important nutrients like protein and antioxidants. Freshwater jellyfish are very sensitive to changes in their environment so human interventions such as pollution, changes in climate and overfishing are very harmful to them. The water tempurature freshwater jellyfish need to survive is 20-25°C (68-77°F.) Thermal pollution can greatly increase water temperature which is very dangerous to the jellyfish.
